  1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

    2. When working as a plumber I knew an apprentice that was under preforming, all the trades-men and even the project managers were telling me he was “useless” and “lazy”. Rather then listen to them and base my judgment of this person off theirs I spent sometime with him and got to know him personally. What I had found out was that he hadn’t lived a easy life and was used to being judged off the colour of his skin. Something I myself have experienced. Because of the relevance his story had to mine I decided to help him the best way I could by explaining what everyone else was saying about him and pushing him to be better. I look back and I’m glad I tried to help him in that situation because I remember when I felt how he did and I had to learn the hard way. I’m glad I was able to help him.

  2. 2. Describe how your culture and how the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

    3. My culture is Samoan. It is a big part and definitely shaped who I am. My culture is big on respect, it feels as if its something that is engrained in you from the moment you’re born. We believe that we should respect each other, respect all around even those that may not give you the same respect back. But most importantly we are taught to respect your elders. That could be your boss, grandparents, but above all your parents as they are the ones that gave you life, they’re the ones that would sacrifice anything and everything for your well-being. I think due to the way I was raised one of the most important things to me is respect, it is something I give to any and everyone until it is abused. Being disrespected is something that hurts me deeply right to my very core.

  5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

    6. Being brown and in the trade can be quite difficult. I experienced a lot of prejudice culture. I was immediately judged by my skin rather then my ability from a lot of the older generation that had been in the trade for most of their life. One strategy I tried was to just put my head down and work as hard as I could to be the best at my job. While that worked it took along time for them to notice and in some cases they wouldn’t notice at all. So I tried another strategy which was to find common ground. My ability to relate to most people helped me greatly. This worked a lot faster as humans are emotional creatures by nature, so by being able to connect with them on a basic emotional level would change their perception of me.
