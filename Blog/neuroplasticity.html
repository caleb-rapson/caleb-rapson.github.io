Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Neuroplasticity – or brain plasticity – is the ability of the brain to modify its connections or re-wire itself. Without this ability, any brain, not just the human brain, would be unable to develop from infancy through to adulthood or recover from brain injury. What makes the brain special is that, unlike a computer, it processes sensory and motor signals in parallel. It has many neural pathways that can replicate another’s function so that small errors in development or temporary loss of function through damage can be easily corrected by rerouting signals along a different pathway.

Some principles are:

  • Use it or lose it: old brain pathways that are no longer in use are “pruned away”.
  • Use it and improve it: the brain can improve with practice.
  • Specificity: the brain can only improve on what it is trained on.
  • Salience matters: the brain pays more attention to things that are important.
  • Transference: the brain can transfer learning from one task to another.

Some of the benefits of neuroplasticity are:

  • It enables learning and memory.
  • It enhances cognitive capabilities.
  • It allows recovery from strokes and brain injuries.
  • It strengthens areas where function is lost or declined.

Discuss what is Growth mindset and why it is relevant

Growth mindset is a belief that intelligence can be developed through effort and experience. It is the opposite of fixed mindset, which is the belief that intelligence is static. People with a growth mindset embrace challenges and persist even when it’s hard. It is relevant to this type of study as coding is constantly changing and developing and if you were to have a fixed mindset you wouldn’t be able to adjust and adapt to the work. Also the growth mindset allows you to preserver even when its hard which is needed as this type of study is constantly getting harder and more complex as the weeks progress.
